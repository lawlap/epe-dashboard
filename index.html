📊 Solar Production Trend — 2024 vs 2025 Analysis (Apr 2026)
Observation: Summer solar generation fell ~22% year-over-year (May−Oct), while winter/spring months are essentially flat (+4%). The biggest drops were May (−32%), Jun (−27%), Jul (−25%), narrowing to Sep (−8%) and Nov (+5%).
Root cause — 2024 was anomalously high, not 2025 low: May 2024 recorded 924 kWh, which is 103% of the theoretical maximum for a 4.6 kW system at Las Cruces peak sun hours. New Mexico was in a significant drought in 2024 with well below-normal cloud cover. 2025 summer production (~70% of theoretical max) reflects more typical conditions.
Why not dirty panels: Soiling in desert environments typically causes 5–7% uniform losses across all months. The seasonal concentration of losses here (32% in summer, near-zero in winter) is inconsistent with soiling or panel degradation, which would affect all months proportionally. The system's strong March 2026 performance (+16% vs prior year) further confirms normal operation.
Recommendation: Annual spring wash (April) is good practice in a dusty desert environment but will not recover the summer gap — that's weather variability. If summer 2026 production tracks 2024 levels, that would further confirm 2025 was the outlier. Monitor inverter logs for fault/clipping events if the trend continues.
System notes: Panels installed December 2019 (30% federal ITC, credited on 2019 return); energized mid-2020 after permitting. Installation was bundled with a new AC system — incremental solar cost was roughly ~$10k out of pocket after credits and other incentives, making standalone payback analysis somewhat notional.
